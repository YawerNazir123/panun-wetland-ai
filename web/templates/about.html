Chatlam Wetland

An AI-powered initiative to document, understand, and protect the migratory and resident bird species of Chatlam Wetland, Kashmir.

About the Wetland

Chatlam Wetland, located in the Pampore region of Kashmir, is a vital freshwater ecosystem that supports a rich diversity of birdlife. It serves as a seasonal refuge for migratory birds arriving from Central Asia, Europe, and northern regions during winter, while also supporting resident species throughout the year.

About This Project

This AI-based Migratory Bird Identification System combines artificial intelligence with biodiversity documentation. Users can explore species records, view images, and identify birds using uploaded photographs or a live camera. All images are stored locally to ensure reliability and offline access.

Currently, the AI model is trained on a selected set of species commonly observed at Chatlam Wetland. Training of remaining species will be carried out in future phases to continuously improve accuracy and coverage.

Contributors & Observers

Bird photographs and observations used in this project are contributed by local birdwatchers, conservationists, and citizen scientists. Their field work plays a crucial role in documenting wetland biodiversity.
